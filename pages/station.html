MAP MY MOUNTAIN

The Table Mountain Aerial Cableway is one of Cape Town's most loved attractions, offering a breathtaking ride to the top of one of the world's natural wonders. The idea for the cableway began in the late 1800s when people dreamed of reaching the mountain's summit without the tough climb. In 1926, Norwegian engineer Trygve Stromsoe and businessman Hendrik Wassenaar brought this dream to life by founding the Table Mountain Aerial Cableway Company. Construction was challenging due to steep cliffs and strong winds, but on 4 October 1929, the cableway officially opened, carrying 25 passengers in small wooden cabins. Over the years, it has been upgraded several times in 1958, 1974, and most notably in 1997 when modern Swiss-made rotating “Rotair” cars were introduced. These cable cars rotate 360 degrees during the five-minute trip, giving visitors stunning views of Cape Town, Table Bay, and the ocean. Since opening, it has safely carried over 30 million visitors and was part of the reason Table Mountain was named one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature in 2012. At the top, visitors can explore nature trails, spot cute dassies (rock hyraxes), and discover over 350 unique plant species. The Table Mountain Café offers tasty meals with a view, while the souvenir shop has perfect keepsakes to remember your trip. There are also free guided tours, photo spots, and sunset rides that make the experience even more magical. Today, the cableway stands as a symbol of innovation, beauty, and adventure a journey every visitor to Cape Town should take
