MAP MY MOUNTAIN

Perched high above Cape Town at 1 067 metres, the Table Mountain Café sits right at the top of the famous Table Mountain Aerial Cableway. Getting there is half the fun you can glide up in a revolving cable car or conquer the climb on foot before being rewarded with incredible panoramic views. The café welcomes you with a cozy indoor area and a sunny outdoor terrace where the city and ocean stretch endlessly below. It's the perfect spot for breakfast, lunch, or just a good cup of coffee after an adventurous hike. The menu serves everything from light sandwiches and burgers to fresh pastries and warm drinks, perfect for refueling while soaking up the view. Though prices are a little higher, the location makes every bite worth it. Crowds gather daily, but the atmosphere buzzes with excitement and the joy of being on top of the world. The Table Mountain Café is not just a restaurant it's an experience. The mountain itself is millions of years old, and the KhoeKhoi people once called it “Hoerikwaggo,” meaning “Mountain of the Sea.” The café's story began in 1929 when the cableway first opened, giving visitors easy access to this wonder. In 1997, upgrades introduced modern revolving cable cars and the café was redesigned to blend comfort with eco-friendly charm. Legends say the mountain's famous “tablecloth” of cloud began during a smoking contest between a pirate and the devil! Today, the café continues to offer warmth, laughter, and a break above the clouds. Supplies are still carried up by cable car, making every cup of coffee a little triumph. Whether you're watching the sunset, escaping the mist, or sharing a meal with friends, it's a moment you'll never forget. Dining here feels like you're part of Cape Town's living postcard. It's history, adventure, and beauty all served on one breathtaking table.
